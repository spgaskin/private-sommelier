# Italy, Spain, Portugal & Germany — Wine Regions

## Italy
Italy has more native grape varieties than any country and DOC/DOCG appellation laws
(DOCG is the top tier; IGT is broader; "Super Tuscan" wines were historically IGT/Vino
da Tavola because they broke DOC rules).

- **Tuscany**: Sangiovese is king. Chianti and Chianti Classico (Gallo Nero/black rooster
  seal), Brunello di Montalcino (100% Sangiovese, long aging), Vino Nobile di Montepulciano.
  Super Tuscans (e.g. Sassicaia, Tignanello) use Bordeaux varieties, often coastal Bolgheri.
- **Piedmont (Piemonte)**: Nebbiolo makes Barolo and Barbaresco — pale but powerful,
  high tannin and acid, tar and roses, age-worthy. Everyday reds from Barbera and Dolcetto.
  Moscato d'Asti is the lightly sweet, low-alcohol sparkler.
- **Veneto**: Prosecco (sparkling, Glera grape, tank method). Soave (white, Garganega).
  Valpolicella and its concentrated dried-grape version Amarone (appassimento method).

## Spain
- **Rioja**: Tempranillo-led reds, classically oak-aged. Aging tiers: Joven (young),
  Crianza, Reserva, Gran Reserva (longest aging).
- **Ribera del Duero**: bold Tempranillo (locally Tinto Fino); Vega Sicilia is the icon.
- **Priorat**: powerful Garnacha/Cariñena reds on llicorella slate soils.
- **Rías Baixas**: crisp Albariño whites (Galicia, Atlantic).
- **Jerez**: Sherry — fortified wine in many styles from dry to sweet: Fino, Manzanilla,
  Amontillado, Oloroso, Palo Cortado, Pedro Ximénez (PX, very sweet). Aged in a solera.
- **Cava**: Spain's traditional-method sparkling, mostly from Penedès (Catalonia).

## Portugal
- **Port (Porto)**: fortified sweet wine from the Douro Valley. Styles: Ruby, Tawny
  (cask-aged, nutty), Late Bottled Vintage (LBV), and Vintage Port (best years, ages decades).
- **Madeira**: fortified, deliberately heated/oxidized, nearly indestructible. Styles by
  grape/sweetness: Sercial (dry) to Malmsey/Malvasia (sweet).
- **Vinho Verde**: light, fresh, slightly spritzy young white.

## Germany
Cool-climate, Riesling heartland. Wines range bone-dry (Trocken) to lusciously sweet.
Prädikat ripeness ladder, driest to sweetest by harvest sugar: Kabinett, Spätlese,
Auslese, Beerenauslese (BA), Trockenbeerenauslese (TBA), and Eiswein (frozen grapes).
Key regions: Mosel (slate soils, delicate low-alcohol Riesling), Rheingau, Pfalz.
Spätburgunder is the German name for Pinot Noir.
