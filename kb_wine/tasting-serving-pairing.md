# Tasting, Serving & Food Pairing

## Serving temperature
- Sparkling & light sweet whites: 6-8 C (well chilled).
- Crisp whites & rosé: 8-11 C.
- Full-bodied whites (oaked Chardonnay): 10-13 C.
- Light reds (Pinot Noir, Beaujolais): 12-14 C (slightly cool).
- Full reds (Cabernet, Syrah, Barolo): 16-18 C ("room temperature" but not warm).
Tip: most reds are served too warm and most whites too cold.

## Glassware & handling
Larger bowls for reds (aeration), smaller for whites, flutes/tulips for sparkling to
preserve bubbles. Hold by the stem to avoid warming the wine. Decanting helps young
tannic reds (aeration) and removes sediment from old reds.

## How to taste (the 5 S's)
See (color, clarity, age — reds brown with age, whites deepen), Swirl (releases aroma),
Smell (fruit, floral, earth, oak, faults), Sip (sweetness, acidity, tannin, body, flavor),
Savor (finish/length). Acidity makes the mouth water; tannin dries the gums; alcohol gives
warmth.

## Common faults
- **Cork taint (TCA)**: wet cardboard / musty smell; the wine is "corked."
- **Oxidation**: flat, sherry-like, browned; too much air exposure.
- **Brett**: barnyard / band-aid aromas (Brettanomyces yeast).
- **Reduction**: struck-match / rotten-egg; often blows off with air.

## Food pairing principles
- Match weight: light wine with light food, bold wine with rich food.
- Acidity cuts fat and refreshes; high-acid wines pair with creamy or fatty dishes.
- Tannin binds to protein and fat: bold reds with red meat; tannin clashes with spicy heat
  and fish (can taste metallic).
- Sweet wine should be at least as sweet as the dessert; also classic with salty/blue cheese
  and spicy food.
- Bubbles and salt: sparkling wine with fried and salty foods works beautifully.

## Quick pairing cheatsheet
- Steak / lamb: Cabernet Sauvignon, Bordeaux, Malbec, Syrah.
- Roast chicken / pork: Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Beaujolais.
- Salmon / tuna: Pinot Noir, dry rosé, oaked Chardonnay.
- White fish / shellfish: Muscadet, Albariño, Chablis, Champagne.
- Tomato pasta: Chianti / Sangiovese (acid matches the sauce).
- Spicy Asian food: off-dry Riesling, Gewürztraminer (sweetness tames heat).
- Hard/aged cheese: Cabernet, vintage Port. Blue cheese: Sauternes, Port.
- Goat cheese / salad: Sauvignon Blanc.

## Aging
Most wine is made to drink young. Age-worthy wines need tannin, acidity, and/or sugar
(structure): top Bordeaux, Barolo, Brunello, Northern Rhône Syrah, German Riesling,
Vintage Port. Over time fruit fades and tertiary notes develop (leather, mushroom, dried
fruit, nuts). Store on the side (if corked), cool (12-14 C), dark, stable, humid.
