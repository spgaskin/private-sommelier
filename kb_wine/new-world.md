# New World — Wine Regions

## United States
California makes about 80% of US wine.
- **Napa Valley**: premium Cabernet Sauvignon (Oakville, Rutherford, Stags Leap, Howell
  Mtn). Rich, ripe, oak-influenced. The 1976 "Judgment of Paris" tasting put Napa Cabernet
  and Chardonnay on the world map by beating French wines in a blind tasting.
- **Sonoma**: cooler and more varied than Napa — Pinot Noir and Chardonnay (Russian River
  Valley), Zinfandel (Dry Creek). Zinfandel is California's signature red (genetically the
  same as Italian Primitivo).
- **Central Coast**: Paso Robles (Rhône blends, Cabernet), Santa Barbara (Pinot Noir).
- **Oregon**: Willamette Valley — cool-climate Pinot Noir, Burgundian in style.
- **Washington State**: Columbia Valley — Cabernet, Merlot, Syrah, Riesling.

## Australia
- **Barossa Valley** (South Australia): big, rich Shiraz; old-vine Grenache. Penfolds Grange
  is the icon.
- **Coonawarra**: Cabernet Sauvignon on terra rossa soil.
- **Margaret River** (Western Australia): Cabernet and Chardonnay.
- **Hunter Valley**: distinctive age-worthy Sémillon.
- **Eden/Clare Valley**: lime-driven dry Riesling.

## New Zealand
- **Marlborough**: world-famous pungent, zesty Sauvignon Blanc (passionfruit, gooseberry).
- **Central Otago**: the world's southernmost major region, excellent Pinot Noir.

## South America
- **Argentina (Mendoza)**: Malbec is the flagship — plush, dark-fruited; high-altitude
  Uco Valley vineyards. Also Torrontés (aromatic white).
- **Chile**: Cabernet and Carmenère (a Bordeaux grape that thrives here) in Maipo,
  Colchagua; cool-climate Sauvignon Blanc and Pinot in Casablanca/San Antonio.

## South Africa
Stellenbosch and surrounds. Chenin Blanc (locally "Steen") is widely planted; Pinotage is
the local crossing of Pinot Noir and Cinsault. Cabernet, Syrah, Sauvignon Blanc also strong.
