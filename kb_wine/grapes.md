# Grape Varieties

## Red varieties
- **Cabernet Sauvignon**: full-bodied, high tannin, blackcurrant, cedar, often oaked.
  Bordeaux Left Bank, Napa, Coonawarra. Ages well.
- **Merlot**: softer, plummy, rounder than Cabernet. Right Bank Bordeaux (Pomerol).
- **Pinot Noir**: light-to-medium body, red cherry, earthy, silky, low tannin. Hard to
  grow. Burgundy, Oregon, Central Otago, Sonoma.
- **Syrah / Shiraz**: peppery and savory in the cool northern Rhône; bold, jammy, sweet-fruited
  as Shiraz in Barossa. Dark fruit, smoke, spice.
- **Grenache / Garnacha**: red fruit, high alcohol, soft tannin; backbone of GSM blends,
  Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Priorat.
- **Malbec**: inky, plush, dark plum; flagship of Argentina (Mendoza); also Cahors in France.
- **Tempranillo**: leather, dried cherry, tobacco; Rioja and Ribera del Duero.
- **Sangiovese**: high acid, tart cherry, herbs; Chianti, Brunello.
- **Nebbiolo**: pale color but huge tannin and acid, tar and roses; Barolo, Barbaresco.
- **Zinfandel / Primitivo**: jammy, brambly, high alcohol; California, Puglia.
- **Cabernet Franc**: leafy, red fruit, graphite; Loire (Chinon), Bordeaux blends.

## White varieties
- **Chardonnay**: the most versatile white — lean and mineral unoaked (Chablis) to rich,
  buttery, oaked (Napa, Meursault). Malolactic fermentation adds a creamy texture.
- **Sauvignon Blanc**: high acid, green/herbaceous, citrus, gooseberry; Marlborough, Sancerre.
- **Riesling**: aromatic, high acid, lime and petrol notes; dry to very sweet; Germany, Alsace,
  Clare Valley. Among the most age-worthy whites.
- **Pinot Grigio / Pinot Gris**: light and crisp as Grigio (Italy); richer, spicier as Gris
  (Alsace).
- **Chenin Blanc**: high acid, honey and quince; dry to sweet to sparkling; Loire, South Africa.
- **Gewürztraminer**: very aromatic, lychee, rose, low acid; Alsace.
- **Viognier**: full-bodied, peachy, floral, low acid; Condrieu (northern Rhône).
- **Albariño**: zesty, saline, stone fruit; Rías Baixas, Vinho Verde.
- **Sémillon**: waxy, low-aromatic; blends with Sauvignon Blanc; key in Sauternes botrytis wines.

## Key terms
- **Tannin**: drying, grippy compound from skins/seeds/oak; structure in red wine.
- **Body**: the wine's weight/fullness on the palate (light, medium, full).
- **Malolactic fermentation (MLF)**: converts sharp malic acid to soft lactic acid; the
  "buttery" character in many Chardonnays.
- **Old World vs New World**: Old World (Europe) tends to earthier, higher-acid, lower-alcohol,
  region-labeled; New World tends to riper, fruitier, higher-alcohol, grape-labeled.
